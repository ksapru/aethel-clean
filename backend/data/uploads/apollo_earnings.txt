Apollo (APO) Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript
Date: Wednesday, May 6, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. ET

CALL PARTICIPANTS
- Marc Rowan - CEO
- James Zelter - President
- Martin Kelly - CFO
- Noah Gunn - Head of Investor Relations

KEY TAKEAWAYS
- Fee-Related Earnings (FRE): $728 million, up 30% year over year.
- Spread-Related Earnings (SRE): $719 million, 6% year-over-year growth.
- Adjusted net income: $1.2 billion, or $1.94 per share.
- Asset Management AUM: Approached $1 trillion, with 31% total growth.
- Origination Volume: $71 billion in the quarter, 25% growth year over year.
- ACS (Capital Solutions) Fees: $246 million, fourth consecutive quarter exceeding $200 million.
- Capital Formation: $115 billion in inflows ($50B organic, $65B from Athora/PIC transaction).
- Global Wealth Fundraising: $4 billion raised during the quarter.
- Daily Pricing Initiative: Apollo will provide daily pricing for 100% of corporate investment grade, direct lending, and asset-backed finance assets platform-wide.
- Secondaries Commentary: Marc Rowan noted, “Across the totality of our $1 trillion platform, secondaries that have been marked up round to 0,” asserting minimal impact of secondary markups on revenue.
- Private Equity Performance: Fund 10 private equity reported 20% net IRR and a 0.4 DPI.

TRANSCRIPT SUMMARY
Management confirmed the near completion of daily pricing roll-out across all corporate investment-grade, direct lending, and asset-backed finance assets. The firm’s capital formation included $65 billion from the Athora-PIC transaction and $50 billion organic, supporting nearly $1 trillion in total assets under management.

Marc Rowan emphasized the focus on fair-value discipline and noted that secondaries markups have minimal impact on overall revenue. The firm is also reducing CLO exposure and growing AMAPS allocations to maintain a defensive posture.
